About

Ahmed Alrehaili is a dedicated academic researcher specializing in emerging technologies that shape the future of digital infrastructure and smart systems.

Currently pursuing his PhD at the University of Sussex (UK), Ahmed serves as a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science at the Islamic University of Madinah (Saudi Arabia). His research bridges theoretical innovation and practical applications in blockchain technology, Internet of Things, and technology acceptance frameworks.

Ahmed holds a Master's degree in Applied Computer Science from Concordia University (Canada, 2012) and a Bachelor's degree from Taibah University (Saudi Arabia, 2008). With over 18 publications and 188+ citations, his work contributes to advancing secure, scalable, and human-centered technological solutions.

Research Focus

Blockchain Technology

Investigating blockchain scalability, data management, and cryptocurrency adoption with focus on practical implementation in various sectors including payment systems and data security.

Internet of Things (IoT)

Developing secure and scalable IoT architectures for smart cities, focusing on edge computing, 5G integration, and resource-constrained device management.

Cloud Computing & Security

Exploring cloud security frameworks, edge computing architectures, and distributed systems with emphasis on performance optimization and data protection.

Technology Acceptance Models

Studying human-centered technology adoption through TAM frameworks, investigating factors that influence user acceptance of emerging technologies in diverse cultural contexts.
